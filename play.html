NAME:Contemporary One-Act Plays

AUthor:B. Roland Lewis

QUIRKE.

There must be great bulk in them indeed.

MRS DELANE.

It is no wonder he to get the job. He must have a great character, so many persons to write for him as what there did.

HYACINTH.

I see no living thing but that thrush——

ANDREW.

I know, I know.

ELLEN.

What! You're really decided——

JOEL.

Hello, Sis!

[Approaching.]

ELLEN.

[Absently.]

Joel; that you?

[Eagerly, following Andrew to the knapsack.]

But, my dear——

ANDREW.

Just these few things, and we're off.

MISSJOYCE.

[Picking it up.]

A half-a-crown! Where, now, did you get that much, Fardy?

FARDY.

Where did I get it, is it?

MISSJOYCE.

I'll engage it was in no honest way you got it.

FARDY.

I picked it up in the street——

MISSJOYCE.

If you did, why didn't you bring it to the sergeant or to his reverence?

MRS DELANE.

And some poor person, maybe, being at the loss of it.
